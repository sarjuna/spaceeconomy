About the Hub

Frugal innovation applied to space

The Space Economy Hub at Cambridge Judge Business School brings together researchers across engineering, economics, governance, and policy to make space activity more accessible — particularly for emerging economies and public-interest missions.

Frugal here means disciplined scoping, reuse, modularity, and explicit cost-risk trade-offs in mission design — not unsafe shortcuts. The hub bridges the gap between technical ambition and practical, governed deployment.

Guiding Principles
  • 1. Do better with less, without compromising safety or reliability.
  • 2. Create repeatable pathways that others can adopt, adapt, and govern.
  • 3. Measure social and economic value alongside technical performance.
The Problem
Why barriers to space participation remain high

Despite falling launch costs and faster innovation cycles, meaningful participation remains concentrated. Entry is still costly, regulated, and uneven — especially for Global South entrants and public-interest missions.

01
High Costs

Launch may be cheaper, but non-recurring engineering, testing, licensing, and insurance remain prohibitive for most new entrants.

02
Complex Rules

Licensing, spectrum, debris, and liability pathways remain fragmented and opaque for teams without dedicated legal infrastructure.

03
Concentrated Capability

Ground stations, integration facilities, and advanced components remain in the hands of a small number of actors.

04
Siloed Thinking

Engineering, economics, governance, and ethics rarely work together early enough in mission design and procurement.

05
Few Shared Tools

Lessons are rarely codified into reusable playbooks, templates, or standards that others can adopt without starting over.

06
Uneven Inclusion

Emerging markets and underserved communities remain secondary in most space narratives, despite having the most to gain.

Research
Cross-disciplinary research agenda

Theory, empirics, and live experiments — making frugal space credible, scalable, and governable across six interconnected programmes.

Programme 01
Frugal Space Systems

How can missions meet performance requirements at radically lower cost through modularity, reuse, and graceful degradation?

Programme 02
Business Models & Finance

Which financing structures support capital-efficient space services in frontier and emerging markets?

Programme 03
Governance & Standards

How can supervised experimentation expand without compromising safety — through sandboxes, model licences, and proportionate regulation?

Programme 04
Social Impact & Ethics

Which use cases create genuine public value — in connectivity, resilience, agriculture, disaster response — and distribute benefits fairly?

Programme 05
Economic Impact

How do missions generate local value, capability building, and wider innovation spillovers across production networks?

Programme 06
Engineering Pathways

COTS trade-offs, low-SWaP payloads, open-hardware reference designs, testing protocols, and sustainable re-entry design.

Outputs
Practical tools, not only publications

The hub produces shared entry infrastructure designed for use by mission teams, regulators, investors, and researchers — creating repeatable pathways for new entrants.

📋
Playbooks

Plain-language guidance on access, regulation, financing, governance, and procurement — covering constellation design, regulatory navigation, and business models.

🔧
Reference Designs & Standards

Open architectures, cost models, COTS validation frameworks, and testing guidance to de-risk technical choices for new entrants.

🗄
Space Data Commons

Flagship datasets — STCFI, CTL-DB, RS-FARM, DISASTER-EO, S2SDG — supporting comparable evidence across missions and geographies.

🧪
Labs & Sandbox Blueprints

Co-design protocols, supervised testing environments, and regulator-ready dossiers for repeatable experimentation within regulatory frameworks.

🎓
Teaching & Capacity Building

Executive education, graduate modules, bootcamps, and regulator training — capacity building as a core hub deliverable.

📊
Annual Impact Dashboard

Reporting against baselines with open analytical pipelines, externally reviewable methods, and decision-useful metrics for funders and policymakers.

Why Cambridge Judge
The combination no one else has

Cambridge Judge is the only business school combining a published frugal innovation base, PNAS-level space economics research, proximity to world-class engineering, and established Global South networks.

Frugal innovation research

Home to foundational frugal innovation research. Radjou and Prabhu's jugaad innovation work originated here, built on a decade of published theory and empirics.

Space economics & policy

Co-directors have published on space technology, space economics, and India's frugal space programme in PNAS — an active, publishing research group.

Business school + engineering

CJBS sits within a university with world-ranked engineering, computer science, and physics. Joint supervision and co-teaching are established practice.

Global South networks

Active links to emerging-market governments, development finance institutions, and the Global South research community through sustained field partnerships.

Regulatory expertise

Faculty work directly with regulators and policymakers. The governance agenda — sandboxes, model licences — maps onto deep Cambridge strengths in law and policy.

Neutral convening authority

No commercial interest, no national space programme agenda, no preferred vendor — a credible honest broker trusted by both Global South and Western agencies.
